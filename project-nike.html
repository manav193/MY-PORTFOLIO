Nike Air Zoom UI

Engineering Case Study

Short Overview

A bold, immersive e-commerce UI concept designed specifically to showcase the dynamic energy of the Nike Air Zoom product line. It challenges traditional, grid-heavy storefronts by placing the product in a dynamic, full-bleed environment.

Problem

Standard e-commerce product pages often feel transactional and static. For high-performance athletic gear, a static white background fails to convey the product's core value proposition: speed, motion, and energy.

My Approach

I treated the interface as an interactive advertisement. The design approach centered on breaking the grid, allowing the shoe imagery to bleed across layout boundaries and interact dynamically with the typography.

Design Decisions

  • Typography: Utilized massive, aggressive, italicized typography to visually communicate forward momentum and speed.
  • Image Masking: Carefully masked the product imagery so that elements of the shoe could overlap the text and UI elements, creating a sense of 3D depth.
  • Color Palette: Stuck to a high-contrast palette—stark blacks, bright whites, and the signature neon accents of the shoe—to maximize visual impact.

Technical Implementation (Design)

The concept was built in Figma, utilizing complex masking layers and blending modes. I prototyped the scroll interactions to ensure that as the user scrolled, the typography and the product image moved at different parallax speeds to amplify the 3D effect.

Challenges

Achieving the perfect balance between artistic expression and e-commerce usability. It was critical to ensure that while the design was bold and grid-breaking, the call-to-action (Add to Cart) and size selectors remained instantly recognizable and accessible.

Result

A high-energy, premium product landing page concept that successfully merges brand storytelling with e-commerce functionality, demonstrating a deep understanding of visual hierarchy and motion design.

Tech Stack

  • Design & Prototyping: Figma
  • Image Editing: Adobe Photoshop

Key Features

  • Grid-breaking 3D layout
  • Kinetic typography
  • Parallax scroll prototyping

Lessons Learned

This project honed my skills in advanced image masking and composition. It proved that e-commerce does not have to be boring, and that pushing boundaries with layout can actually enhance the perceived value of a product.
